How to Use AgRes Suite

A step-by-step guide to running CRD, RBD, and LSD experimental design analysis — from uploading your data to interpreting the ANOVA output. Follow along with the screenshots for each stage.

1

Prepare & Upload Your Data

Arrange your data in long format — one observation per row, with column headers in the first row. Save as CSV or Excel (.xlsx). Your file needs a treatment column, a response (measurement) column, and design-specific columns:

DesignRequired columns
CRDTreatment, Response
RBDTreatment, Replication, Response
LSDTreatment, Row, Column, Response

Click the upload box or drag your file in. To explore first, click Load Sample Data for a ready-made rice variety trial.

💡 Tip: Keep treatment names consistent (e.g. always "IR 20", not "IR20" in some rows). The tool groups by exact text match.
2

Select Design & Map Columns

Once uploaded, a preview of your data appears. Choose your experimental design from the three tabs:

  • CRD — Completely Randomized Design. Use when experimental units are homogeneous (e.g. pots, lab conditions).
  • RBD — Randomized Block Design. Use when you have a known source of variation to block (e.g. field gradient). Reveals a Replication column field.
  • LSD — Latin Square Design. Controls variation in two directions. Reveals Row and Column fields. Requires a perfect p×p square.

Then map each dropdown to the matching column in your data. Select one or more response variables (hold Ctrl/Cmd to pick several — each gets its own ANOVA).

3

Run the Analysis

Click Run Analysis. The tool computes everything locally in your browser — your data never leaves your device. A green success message confirms completion and the results appear below automatically.

4

Read the ANOVA Table

The ANOVA table shows the variance breakdown. Key columns:

  • F Value — ratio of treatment to error variance. Higher means treatments differ more.
  • Pr(>F) — the p-value. Below 0.05 means significant treatment differences.
  • Sig — significance stars: *** (p<0.001), ** (p<0.01), * (p<0.05), NS (not significant).

Below the table, the statistics box gives Grand Mean, CV%, SE(m), SE(d), and Critical Difference (CD) at 5% and 1%.

💡 CV% indicates experiment precision. Below 10% is excellent; 10–20% is acceptable for field trials; above 20% suggests high variability.
5

Interpret Means & DMRT Groupings

The treatment means table ranks treatments from highest to lowest, each tagged with a DMRT letter group. Treatments sharing a letter are not significantly different; treatments with no shared letter differ significantly. The bar chart visualizes this — hover any bar to see its group.

Generate AI Interpretation & Export

Click Generate AI Interpretation for a plain-language, thesis-ready writeup of your results — ANOVA summary, treatment comparison, best performer, and conclusion. Then use Copy ANOVA or Download CSV to save your results for your report.
